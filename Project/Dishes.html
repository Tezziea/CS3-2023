Dishes

Sinigang

Sinigang is a popular Filipino soup known for its distinctive sour flavor, which is achieved through the use of a souring agent. The soup typically consists of a variety of meats (such as pork, beef, shrimp, or fish) and a selection of vegetables, all simmered in a tamarind-based broth. The sourness of the tamarind gives sinigang its unique taste.

Variants of this dish:

Sinigang na manok

Sinigang na isda

Sinigang na baboy

Sinigang na hipon

Adobo

The national dish of the Philippines, which consists of chunks of meat, fish, or vegetables, marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices and then stewed in the marinade.

Variants of this dish:

Adobong manok

Adobong baboy

Laing

Laing is a Filipino dish of shredded or whole taro leaves with meat or seafood cooked in thick coconut milk spiced with labuyo chili, lemongrass, garlic, shallots, ginger, and shrimp paste.

Variants of this dish:

Inulukan

Tinumok

La Paz Batchoy

La Paz Batchoy is a Filipino noodle soup of pork offal, crushed pork cracklings, chicken stock, beef loin, and round noodles. The original and most popular variant, the La Paz batchoy, traces its roots to the Iloilo City district of La Paz, in the Philippines. La Paz Batchoy.
